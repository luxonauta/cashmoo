Create a New Goal

Financial goals give you direction and motivation. Whether you're saving for vacation, building an emergency fund, or planning for retirement, setting clear targets helps turn dreams into reality.

Goal Details

Give your goal a motivating name that reminds you what you're working toward. Keep it under 50 characters.

How much money do you want to save? Enter the total amount you need to reach your goal.

Choose whether this is something you want to achieve each month or once per year. This helps track your progress appropriately.

Goal Setting Tips

  • Start small: Your first goal doesn't need to be huge. Even saving $100 builds the habit and confidence.
  • Be specific: "Emergency fund of $1,000" is better than "save more money" because you know exactly when you've succeeded.
  • Make it realistic: Set targets you can actually achieve with your current income and expenses.
  • Monthly vs Annual: Use monthly for regular savings habits, annual for larger one-time purchases or yearly targets.

Popular Goal Ideas

Need inspiration? Here are some common financial goals to consider:

Emergency Fund

Save 3-6 months of expenses for unexpected situations like job loss or medical bills. Start with $1,000 as your first milestone.

Vacation Fund

Set aside money monthly for your dream trip. A $2,400 annual goal means saving $200 per month.

Down Payment

Saving for a house? Most lenders want 10-20% down. Calculate what you need and break it into monthly targets.

Debt Freedom

Set a goal to pay off credit cards or loans. Focus on high-interest debt first to save money on interest charges.
