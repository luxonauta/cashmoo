Smart Credit Card Usage

  • Keep utilization low: Use less than 30% of your credit limit, ideally under 10%, to maintain a good credit score.
  • Pay on time: Late payments hurt your credit score and result in expensive fees and interest charges.
  • Pay in full: Paying the full balance each month avoids interest charges and builds good credit history.
  • Know your dates: Understanding when your statement closes helps you manage your credit utilization timing.

Credit Health Guidelines

Follow these guidelines to build and maintain excellent credit:

Credit Utilization

Keep total credit usage below 30% of your limits. Lower is better - under 10% is ideal for excellent credit scores.

Payment History

Pay at least the minimum on time, every time. Payment history is 35% of your credit score - the most important factor.

Statement Timing

Your balance when the statement closes is what gets reported to credit bureaus, affecting your utilization ratio.

Multiple Cards

Having multiple cards can help your credit mix and available credit, but only if you can manage them responsibly.

Important Reminders

  • Credit cards are a tool for building credit and managing cash flow, not for spending money you don't have
  • Interest rates on credit cards are typically very high - avoid carrying balances when possible
  • Missing payments can result in late fees, higher interest rates, and damage to your credit score
  • Only use credit cards if you have a plan to pay them off responsibly
